1. Biology is a word that is derived from two Greek words bios and logos.
a) Bios means life
b) Logos means knowledge
Therefore biology can be defined as branch of science that deals with the study of living things. The aim of biology is to explain the living world in terms of scientific principles. Living things interacts with non-living things in their physical environment. Therefore, the study of biology has been explained to the study of non- living things and the role of human beings in shaping their environment .

2. Geography is a word that was derived from two Greek words Geo and Graphein.
a)Geo which means earth and,
b)Graphein which means to write, to draw; write about or describe the earth.
Geography is study of description of the distribution of the natural features of the earth and their inter-relationship of various phenomena on the earth surface due to human activities and how they affects man.

Biogeography is the study of the spatial patterns of biological diversity and its causes, both in the present and in the past. Bio-geographers’ synthesize information from a very broad range of fields, including ecology, evolution, paleontology, and climatology. It is a branch of geography that studies the past and present distribution of the world's many species. It is usually considered to be a part of physical geography as it often relates to the examination of the physical environment and how it affects species and shaped their distribution across space. Biogeography has strong ties to biology, ecology, evolution studies, climatology, and soil science. Biogeography is a branch of natural sciences that describes the distribution and abundance of living organisms. It encompasses patterns and processes that operate across a wide range of spatial and temporal scales and therefore attempts to answer different questions such as ‘where do living organisms occur, why do they occur where they occur, how many are they and what do they do?’ in other words biogeography deals with identification of different patterns of distribution of organisms on or beneath the earth’s surface.
